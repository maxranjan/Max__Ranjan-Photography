Photography is a way of feeling, of touching, of loving.
What you have caught on film is captured forever...
it remembers little things, long after you have forgotten everything.
When a moment in front of me appears to be particularly special,
whether it be by beauty or experience, I capture it.
I usually find a reason to justify taking that photo - symmetry,
or color or contrast - and it's my hope that my photography sheds
light onto what I see and do on a daily basis.



Top 3 Photographers in India


Raghunath Rai Chowdhry (Raghu Rai) is the top photographer in India
and a photojournalist who was born in 1942. Rai was a protégé of Henri Cartier-Bresson, who hired him to Magnum Photos in 1977 as a young photojournalist.
He left the newspaper in 1976 to work as a freelance photographer. The Indian government honored him with the Padmashree award for his photography work.


Atul Kasbekar is an Indian fashion photographer and Bollywood
film producer who was born on April 22, 1965. His Kingfisher Calendar shoots have made him famous. In addition, he served as honorary chairman of the Photographer’s
Guild of India. Since its inception in 2003, Atul Kasbekar’s work for Kingfisher Calendar has been well-known. Currently,
He is also a film producer and always on the list of the top photographers of India.


Dayanita Singh is an Indian photographer who specializes in book photography.
She has fourteen books to her credit. Singh’s work examines and draws on how people interact with visual photographs.
Her portraits of India’s metropolitan elite and middle class are her most well-known works.
